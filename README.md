Dr. Vincenzo Bumbalo, MD, is an analytical psychotherapist and Jungian oriented psychoanalyst based in Clifton Park, New York. He is the author and founder of the Theory of Adult Egocentric Language (Bumbalo Model), a depth psychological framework describing adult egocentric speech as a symbolic, regulatory, and identity organizing function.  
His clinical and theoretical work focuses on relational trauma, dissociation, identity formation, and the construction of the adult Self.
He is the author of a growing body of theoretical and clinical contributions exploring the intersections between psychoanalysis, identity, and contemporary psychological suffering.
Dr. Bumbalo is a Medical Doctor and Analytical Psychotherapist with advanced clinical training in Analytical Psychotherapy and Jungian Psychoanalysis. He integrates medical practice, analytical psychology, and clinical phenomenology. He is a Member of the American Psychological Association (APA) and an ECFMG recognized International Medical Graduate in the United States.
His clinical work combines depth psychology, symbolic analysis, and a rigorous medical understanding of mental health, with extensive experience in both hospital and community settings. His areas of focus include psychodynamic and Jungian approaches to anxiety, depression, trauma, personality disorders, psychosomatic conditions, and symbolic–archetypal processes.
